Are You Fast Enough?
MOTORSPORT RACING

Do you think you have enough for the high-stakes adrenaline rush of motorsport racing? Lets find out! Motorsport racing is a sport that goes back to the invention of the car. From its inception, people always wanted to go faster down the straights and quicker through the corners. From bootlegging alcohol to outrunning police, motorsport racing has some nefarious roots. Do you have enough to make the cut? Lets calculate your theoretical lap time to see where you stand.

CHOOSE A TRACK

  • The Nurburgring

    With 170 corners spanning across 12.94 miles, this race track is highly coveted by all who visit. Regailed as the holy grail of racetracks, only experienced racers escape its corners unscathed. Do you have what it takes?

  • Pikes Peak

    156 Corners. 14,115 feet above the sea. Devestating to cars and drivers alike.

  • 24 Hours of Le Mans

    Famous for the Mulsanne Straight, this track is 8.5 miles of top speed heaven. Built for the purpose of constant maximum velocity, corners are a figment of the imagination. Why worry about turning when you can catch your opponent in the straight line - that is, if you have a lead foot and no fear of crashing

CHOOSE A CAR

  • VOLKSWAGEN I.D.R.

    An electric Frankenstein made for the corners but gutless on the straights. Though its top speed is abysmal, it's instant electrical torque make it a devil on any pavement that bends.

  • Peugot Hillclimb

    With twin turbo chargers, and no shortage of horsepower, this vehicle was made to never run out of breath, even as the air around it thins. Ups, downs, left, right, this car can take any direction its driver puts it in.

  • NASCAR

    Whats there to say? All American Muscle Baby. Corners are irrelevant when you have a Detroit V8 under the hood. The old saying holds true: no replacement for displacement.

WEATHER CONDITIONS

  • Hot N Dry

    The perfect condition for any race or car, this is every racers secret dream.

  • Hot N Humid

    A rare mix that sometimes occurs, it separates the rookies from the pros who can navigate this weather - and sometimes excel

  • Cold N Wet

    Hard on the tires, hard on the car, hard on the soul. You need mental fortitude to race in these conditions.

  • Snow

    More than just slippery, do not attempt unless you have experience or a death wish
